The Legacy of Squash

Squash was invented in the 1830s at Harrow School in England when students discovered that a punctured Rackets ball, which "squashed" on impact with the wall, produced a game with a greater variety of shots. From the stone courtyards of London to the high-tech glass arenas of today, squash has become a global phenomenon, recently recognized as an Olympic sport for the LA 2028 Games.
